# Zitterbewegung (ZBW) Oscillation Frequency and Phases (Paper 1 Section 10)

Zitterbewegung (ZBW) is the fundamental oscillatory mode of Dipole Pairs (DPs) in the Dipole Sea, occurring at frequency f_ZBW ≈ 1/(2 t_Pl), where t_Pl is the Planck time (~5.39 × 10^{-44} s). This corresponds to one full cycle over two Planck times: attraction phase + repulsion phase.

## Cycle Mechanics
1. **Attraction Phase**  
   When the +limb of a DP is far from a negative CP (or sea baseline), the SSV gradient is strongest inward → +limb moves toward the gradient.

2. **Cancellation Phase**  
   As the +limb nears the negative CP, SSVs point opposite and sum toward zero → net inward gradient drops.

3. **Repulsion Phase**  
   The +limb's outer side now feels the stronger sea baseline SSV (away from center) → gradient inverts → repulsion outward.

4. **Reset**  
   Distance increases → inward gradient re-dominates → cycle repeats.

## Key Properties
- Frequency: f_ZBW ≈ 9.5 × 10^{43} Hz (one cycle per 2 t_Pl)
- Period: ~1.078 × 10^{-43} s
- Amplitude: r_eff ~ Compton wavelength / φ (golden ratio scaling from lattice)
- Energy: Kinetic contribution ½ m v² (relativistic v ≈ c in inner regions)
- Role: Sustains polarization against entropy, generates spin (orbital), adds mass (linear/unbound)
