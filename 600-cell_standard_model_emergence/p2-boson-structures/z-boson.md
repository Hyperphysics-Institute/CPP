# Z Boson: Icosahedral Cage Structure (Appendix D.2)

The Z boson is an icosahedral cage (180 effective vertices) with inter-layer bonding, no central unpaired CP, and uniform 25% DP mix.

## Structural Details
- Composition: Symmetric cage of hDPs/eDPs/qDPs (25% each) for neutrality.
- Closed structure: Minimizes external SSV gradients → unreactive, clean decays.
- ZBW: Delocalized oscillations across cage vertices → mass from inter-bonding and cloud.
- Production: High-energy SSV concentrations nucleate cage from sea fluctuations (e.g., q q̄ → Z).

## Mass Contribution
- E_inter dominant (bonding between vertices).
- E_cloud from diffuse polarization.
- Observed: 91.1876 GeV/c².

## Role
Neutral mediator of weak neutral currents. Symmetric decays (e.g., ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ pairs) reflect balanced DP mix.
