# Higgs Boson: Dodecahedral Cloud Structure

The Higgs boson is a dodecahedral cage (3000 effective vertices) with surrounding polarized DP cloud, no central unpaired CP, and uniform 25% DP mix.

## Structural Details
- Composition: Diffuse condensation of DPs in dodecahedral symmetry.
- No central CP → highest boson mass from large-scale organization.
- ZBW: Collective fluctuations across cage and cloud → mass from polarization energy.
- Production: Triggered by strong SSV localization (e.g., gluon fusion, vector boson fusion).

## Mass Contribution
- E_cloud dominant (diffuse polarization).
- E_inter from cage bonding.
- Observed: 125.25 GeV/c².

## Role
Provides mass to W/Z via interaction with sea condensate; symmetric decays reflect neutral structure.
