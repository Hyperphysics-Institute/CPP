# Neutrino Flavor Structures: Unbound Orbital ZBW Configurations

Neutrinos lack a central unpaired Conscious Point (CP) and derive mass from self-sustained orbital ZBW of minimal DP structures with d=3 unbound dimensions (σ ≈ 5.8 × 10^{-7}).

## Flavor-Specific Structures
- **ν_e (electron neutrino)**  
  Simplest: single orbital eDP (electron dipole pair). Minimal organization → lowest SSV coherence → smallest mass (~0.001 eV).

- **ν_μ (muon neutrino)**  
  Single orbital qDP (quark dipole pair). Moderate resonance from qCP-like structure → stronger organization → intermediate mass (~0.004 eV).

- **ν_τ (tau neutrino)**  
  Tetrahedral cluster of hybrid Dipole Pairs (hDP-tetra: 4 interconnected hDPs, each bound to three others). Highest organizational demand (overlapping oscillations) → largest mass (~0.012 eV).

## Common Properties
- Unbound orbital mode: self-contained circular motion, no central anchor → maximal entropy dilution.
- Energy contribution: E_spin = ½ m v² · σ (v ∼ c / r_k, but tiny due to suppression).
- Stability: hDP-tetra balances attraction/repulsion (all-eDP unstable, all-qDP collapses).
