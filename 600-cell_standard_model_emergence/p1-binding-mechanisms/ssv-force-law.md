# SSV Gradient Force Law (Paper 1 Section 3)

The Space Stress Vector (SSV) is the fundamental interaction field in CPP, generated by CPs and propagating along lattice hyperedge pathways.

## SSV Field at a Grid Point (Equation 1)
The SSV at any GP is the vector sum of contributions from all CPs within its Planck Sphere Radius (PSR):

\[
\vec{\text{SSV}}_{\text{GP}} = \sum_{i \in \text{PSR}_i} \frac{\text{SSV}_{0,i} \cdot p_i \cdot t_i \cdot f(\text{type compatibility}) \cdot \hat{r}_{i \to \text{GP}}}{r_{i \to \text{GP}}^2}
\]

Parameters:
- SSV_{0,i}: Elementary stress magnitude from CP i (derived as 0.2555 MeV in Section 8)
- p_i: Polarity (+1 or −1)
- t_i: Type factor (1 for eCP, 0.5 for qCP due to rotational symmetry)
- f(type compatibility): Modulates strength (stronger for qCP resonance)
- \hat{r}_{i \to \text{GP}}: Unit vector
- r_{i \to \text{GP}}: Lattice distance (d_0 = 1 = discrete Planck increment)

## Force on Test CP (Equation 2)
The force on a test CP j at position \vec{r}_j is:

\[
\vec{F}_j = -q_j \cdot \vec{\text{SSV}}(\vec{r}_j)
\]

where q_j = +1 or −1. Opposite polarities attract; same repel. Type compatibility modulates coupling.

## Potential Energy
The total binding energy of a cage is the negative of the pairwise SSV potential (factor 1/2 avoids double-counting):

\[
E_{\text{binding}} = -\frac{1}{2} \sum_{j} q_j \cdot \Phi(\vec{r}_j)
\]

with potential at \vec{r}_j:

\[
\Phi(\vec{r}_j) = \sum_{i \neq j} \frac{\text{SSV}_{0,i} \cdot p_i \cdot t_i \cdot f(\text{type compatibility})}{|\vec{r}_i - \vec{r}_j|}
\]

Stable cages minimize E_binding (maximize negative magnitude) by aligning polarities/types along lattice symmetries.
