## Three Critical Questions Requiring Thomas's Input

### Question 1: What is a Dipole Particle (DP)?

**Options:**
- A bound CP pair (one + and one −)?
- If so, what provides the binding: pure Coulomb attraction or geometric constraint from 600-cell?
- What sets the equilibrium separation between the CP constituents?

**Why this matters:** The stiffness derivation depends on the internal structure and binding mechanism.

### Question 2: What is the equilibrium configuration of the DP sea?

**Candidate equilibrium states:**
- Maximum entropy (random DP orientations)  
- Minimum energy (all DPs aligned)
- Geometric constraint (DPs oriented along 600-cell edges)

**Why this matters:** Stiffness \(C\) is the second derivative of energy with respect to displacement from equilibrium. We need to know what equilibrium looks like to calculate the second derivative.

### Question 3: What is the CP-DP interaction mechanism?

**Working hypothesis:** Coulombic coupling
- Free CP polarizes surrounding DPs through charge attraction
- This polarization *is* the SSV field
- Coupling strength determined by Coulomb's law at Planck scale

**Derivation pathway:**
If DP separation is \(l_P\) and coupling is Coulombic:

$$U = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 \cdot l_P} = \frac{\alpha\hbar c}{l_P}$$

where \(\alpha \approx 1/137\) is the fine structure constant.

Stiffness = second derivative of this potential ∼ \(E_P/l_P^3\) = \(\text{SSV}_{\text{crit}}\)

The geometric factor \(\alpha_{\text{geom}} \approx 0.5594\) accounts for 600-cell icosahedral symmetry reducing effective stiffness from isotropic Coulomb value.
