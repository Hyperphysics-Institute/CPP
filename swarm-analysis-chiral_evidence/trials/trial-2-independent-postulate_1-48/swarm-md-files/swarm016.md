# Swarm016: Quasar Absorption Line Velocity Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic velocity asymmetries in quasar broad absorption line profiles
- **Scale**: Active Galactic Nuclei Physics (accretion disk winds)
- **Experimental Context**: High-resolution optical and UV spectroscopy
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Quasar broad absorption lines exhibit systematic asymmetries in their velocity profiles, with blue and red wings showing different absorption depths and velocity extents beyond simple outflow geometry expectations. These asymmetries manifest as correlations between absorption line strength and velocity offset that vary systematically across different ionic species, potentially indicating complex accretion disk wind structures or radiative transfer effects in relativistic outflows.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Broad absorption line regions in luminous quasars
- **Instruments**: Hubble Space Telescope STIS/COS, VLT UVES, Keck HIRES
- **Method**: High-resolution UV/optical spectroscopy with velocity profile analysis
- **Observable**: Absorption line velocity asymmetry A_abs
- **Precision**: Velocity uncertainties ~5-20 km/s depending on line strength and resolution
- **Controls**: Instrumental profile corrections, blending line effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Broad absorption line velocity asymmetry A_abs
- **Definition**: A_abs = (W_blue - W_red)/(W_blue + W_red) for blue/red wing equivalent widths
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 4-7σ significance for strong absorption systems
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric outflow model predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻¹ to 10⁰ relative equivalent width asymmetry depending on ionization state
