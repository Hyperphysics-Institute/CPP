# Swarm027: Asteroid Rotation Pole Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Statistical asymmetries in asteroid rotation pole orientations relative to orbital plane and ecliptic coordinates
- **Scale**: Solar System Dynamics (small body physics)
- **Experimental Context**: Photometric lightcurve analysis and radar observations
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Main belt asteroids exhibit systematic asymmetries in their rotation pole orientations that deviate from random distributions expected from collisional evolution models. These asymmetries manifest as preferred pole directions relative to the ecliptic plane, correlations between pole orientation and asteroid family membership, and statistical clustering that exceeds expectations from isotropic spin vector distributions, potentially indicating YORP effect influences, primordial formation signatures, or systematic collisional processes.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Rotation properties of main belt asteroids from photometric and radar observations
- **Instruments**: Ground-based photometric surveys, Arecibo/Goldstone radar, space mission data
- **Method**: Lightcurve inversion and radar shape modeling for spin vector determination
- **Observable**: Pole orientation asymmetry A_pole from statistical distribution analysis
- **Precision**: Pole direction uncertainties ~5-20 degrees depending on observation quality
- **Controls**: Selection biases, observational geometry effects
