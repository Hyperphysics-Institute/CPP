### Physical Description
Neutron beta decay exhibits systematic asymmetries in the angular correlations between decay electrons and neutrinos that deviate from standard V-A weak interaction predictions in specific kinematic regions. These asymmetries manifest as preferred relative orientations between electron and neutrino momentum vectors, correlations with nuclear spin orientations, and systematic deviations in correlation coefficients that exceed expected statistical fluctuations, potentially indicating beyond Standard Model physics, tensor weak interaction components, or systematic experimental effects in precision decay measurements.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Free neutron beta decay with electron and neutrino detection
- **Instruments**: UCNA experiment at LANSCE, Nab experiment design, aSPECT at ILL

- **Observable**: Beta decay asymmetry parameter A_beta from correlation coefficient deviations
- **Precision**: Correlation coefficient uncertainties ~10⁻³ to 10⁻⁴ depending on statistics
- **Controls**: Detector systematic effects, neutron beam polarization, background subtraction
