### Physical Description
Solar wind plasma exhibits systematic asymmetries in magnetic field turbulence helicity that manifest as persistent negative helicity excess across multiple solar cycles and heliocentric distances. These asymmetries correlate with solar rotation, coronal mass ejection properties, and stream interaction regions, showing reproducible handedness preferences in turbulent cascade processes that exceed expectations from isotropic turbulence models, potentially indicating large-scale solar dynamo effects, Alfvén wave propagation asymmetries, or systematic coronal field topology influences on heliospheric turbulence.

- **Precision**: Magnetic field measurement uncertainties ~0.1 nT with high temporal resolution
- **Controls**: Instrument calibration, coordinate system effects, solar cycle variations
