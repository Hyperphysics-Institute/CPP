### Physical Description
Comet nuclei exhibit systematic hemispheric asymmetries in their outgassing patterns that manifest as differences in volatile production rates, jet activity, and surface composition between northern and southern hemispheres. These asymmetries exceed expectations from simple sublimation models and correlate with orbital parameters and seasonal illumination cycles, potentially indicating heterogeneous nucleus composition, subsurface structure variations, or complex thermal evolution processes during multiple perihelion passages.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Volatile outgassing from comet nuclei during active phases
- **Instruments**: Rosetta/OSIRIS imaging, ALMA millimeter observations, ground-based spectroscopy
- **Method**: High-resolution imaging and spectroscopic monitoring of gas production rates
- **Observable**: Outgassing asymmetry parameter A_gas from hemispheric production rate ratios
- **Precision**: Gas production uncertainties ~10-30% depending on species and heliocentric distance
- **Controls**: Viewing geometry effects, seasonal illumination variations
