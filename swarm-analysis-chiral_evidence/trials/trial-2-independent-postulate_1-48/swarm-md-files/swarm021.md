# Swarm021: Fast Radio Burst Polarization Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in fast radio burst polarization properties and arrival time distributions
- **Scale**: Extragalactic Transient Physics (coherent radio emission)
- **Experimental Context**: Radio telescope FRB detection and polarimetry
- **Time Period**: 2016-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Fast radio bursts exhibit systematic asymmetries in their linear polarization properties and temporal structure that correlate with dispersion measure and burst morphology. These asymmetries manifest as preferred polarization angles, asymmetric pulse profiles, and correlations between polarization degree and burst energetics that exceed expectations from simple coherent emission models, potentially indicating complex magnetic field geometries or propagation effects in extreme plasma environments.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Millisecond-duration coherent radio bursts from extragalactic sources
- **Instruments**: CHIME/FRB, ASKAP/CRAFT, Parkes, Arecibo (historical), VLA real-time system
- **Method**: High-time-resolution polarimetry with statistical population analysis
- **Observable**: FRB polarization asymmetry A_pol and temporal asymmetry A_temp
- **Precision**: Polarization uncertainties ~1-10% depending on burst signal-to-noise
- **Controls**: Instrumental polarization calibration, ionospheric effects
