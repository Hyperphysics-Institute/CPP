# Swarm007: Galaxy Rotation Curve Velocity Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in galaxy rotation curve velocities between approaching and receding sides
- **Scale**: Galactic Dynamics (dark matter/modified gravity)
- **Experimental Context**: Radio astronomy HI velocity field mapping
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Spiral galaxies exhibit systematic velocity asymmetries in their rotation curves, where the approaching and receding sides show different velocity profiles beyond expectations from simple axisymmetric rotation models. These asymmetries manifest as consistent deviations in HI 21cm line velocities that correlate with galaxy morphology, potentially indicating non-trivial dark matter distribution asymmetries or modified gravity effects.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: HI gas velocity fields in spiral galaxies
- **Instruments**: Arecibo Legacy Fast ALFA Survey, THINGS VLA survey, MeerKAT survey data
- **Method**: Radio interferometry velocity field mapping and kinematic modeling
- **Observable**: Approaching-receding velocity asymmetry A_vel
- **Precision**: Velocity uncertainties ~2-5 km/s with high-resolution mapping
- **Controls**: Beam smearing corrections, inclination angle systematic effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Rotation curve velocity asymmetry A_vel

- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-5σ significance for individual well-resolved galaxies
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric rotation model predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻² to 10⁻¹ relative velocity asymmetry depending on galaxy mass and morphology
