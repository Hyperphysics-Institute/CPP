# Swarm036: Enceladus Geysers Eruption Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in Enceladus geyser eruption patterns and plume compositions between tiger stripe regions
- **Scale**: Planetary Geology Physics (cryovolcanism)
- **Experimental Context**: Cassini spacecraft observations and thermal imaging
- **Time Period**: 2015-2017 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Enceladus exhibits systematic asymmetries in geyser activity between different tiger stripe fractures and within individual tiger stripes that correlate with tidal phase, subsurface thermal structure, and proximity to the south polar terrain boundary. These asymmetries manifest as differences in eruption intensity, plume particle size distributions, and chemical compositions between eastern and western sectors of the south polar region, potentially indicating asymmetric subsurface heating, variable fracture connectivity to the subsurface ocean, or complex tidal stress distributions in the ice shell.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Water vapor and ice particle geysers from Enceladus south polar tiger stripes
- **Instruments**: Cassini CIRS thermal mapping, ISS imaging, INMS composition analysis
- **Method**: Multi-instrument analysis of geyser activity with tidal phase correlation
- **Observable**: Geyser eruption asymmetry A_geyser from activity and composition variations
- **Precision**: Thermal emission uncertainties ~1-5 K, particle flux ~10-30% depending on geometry
- **Controls**: Orbital phase effects, instrument viewing geometry, seasonal illumination
