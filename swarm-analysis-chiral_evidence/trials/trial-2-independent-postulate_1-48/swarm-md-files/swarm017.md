# Swarm017: Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Large-scale asymmetries in cosmic microwave background polarization patterns
- **Scale**: Cosmological (primordial universe)
- **Experimental Context**: Full-sky CMB polarization measurements
- **Time Period**: 2015-2025 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
The cosmic microwave background exhibits systematic asymmetries in its polarization patterns across the sky that exceed expectations from standard cosmological models with statistical isotropy. These asymmetries manifest as preferred orientations in the E-mode and B-mode polarization fields and correlations between polarization direction and large-scale temperature anisotropies, potentially indicating primordial asymmetries, topological defects, or systematic instrumental effects.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: CMB polarization from the surface of last scattering at z ≈ 1100
- **Instruments**: Planck satellite, WMAP, ground-based telescopes (SPT, ACT, BICEP/Keck)
- **Method**: Full-sky polarization mapping with spherical harmonic decomposition
- **Observable**: Polarization asymmetry parameters A_E, A_B for E/B modes
- **Precision**: Polarization sensitivity ~μK-arcmin with systematic control
- **Controls**: Foreground subtraction, instrumental polarization systematics
