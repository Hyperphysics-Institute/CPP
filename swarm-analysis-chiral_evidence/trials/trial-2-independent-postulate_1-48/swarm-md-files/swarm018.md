# Swarm018: Large-Scale Structure Void Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Statistical asymmetries in cosmic void shapes and orientations in large-scale structure
- **Scale**: Cosmological Structure Formation (dark matter halos)
- **Experimental Context**: Galaxy redshift surveys and N-body simulations
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Cosmic voids in the large-scale structure exhibit systematic asymmetries in their shapes, orientations, and galaxy distributions that exceed expectations from standard ΛCDM cosmology with Gaussian initial conditions. These asymmetries manifest as preferred void elongation directions, non-random orientations relative to surrounding filaments, and correlations between void properties and large-scale tidal fields, potentially indicating primordial non-Gaussianity or modified gravity effects.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Cosmic voids identified in galaxy distribution surveys
- **Surveys**: SDSS DR16, BOSS/eBOSS, Dark Energy Survey, Euclid early data
- **Method**: Void finding algorithms with shape and orientation analysis
- **Observable**: Void shape asymmetry A_void and orientation correlation
- **Precision**: Void ellipticity uncertainties ~0.05-0.1 depending on void size
- **Controls**: Survey selection effects, void finder systematic biases

**Primary Observable**: Void shape asymmetry A_void

- **Scale**: ~10⁻² to 10⁻¹ relative shape asymmetry depending on void mass and environment
