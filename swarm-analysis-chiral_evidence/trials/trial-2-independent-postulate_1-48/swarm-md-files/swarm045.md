# Swarm045: Pulsar Glitch Recovery Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in pulsar post-glitch recovery patterns between spin-up and spin-down phases
- **Scale**: Neutron Star Physics (stellar dynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: Radio telescope timing observations
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Pulsar timing observations reveal systematic asymmetries in post-glitch recovery behavior that correlate with glitch magnitude, pulsar age, and magnetic field strength. These asymmetries manifest as differences in exponential decay timescales between initial rapid recovery and long-term secular evolution, correlations between recovery asymmetry and pulsar characteristic age, and systematic deviations from symmetric vortex unpinning models, potentially indicating superfluid turbulence effects, crustal stress redistribution asymmetries, or systematic timing analysis effects in neutron star interior dynamics.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Rotation timing of radio pulsars following glitch events
- **Instruments**: Parkes Pulsar Timing Array, Jodrell Bank Observatory, FAST telescope
- **Method**: High-precision pulsar timing with glitch detection and recovery analysis
- **Observable**: Pulsar glitch recovery asymmetry A_glitch from timing residual analysis
- **Precision**: Timing residual uncertainties ~10 ns to 1 μs depending on pulsar brightness
- **Controls**: Interstellar medium effects, clock corrections, instrumental systematics
