## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

- **Scale**: Stellar Evolution Physics (asymptotic giant branch mass loss)

### Physical Description
Planetary nebulae exhibit systematic morphological asymmetries that manifest as point-reflection symmetry breaking, asymmetric ionization structures, and correlations between nebular geometry and central star properties. These asymmetries exceed expectations from spherically symmetric mass loss models and show statistical preferences for specific orientation patterns, potentially indicating binary evolution effects, magnetic field influences, or asymmetric stellar wind interactions during the asymptotic giant branch phase.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Ionized gas shells around central white dwarf stars
- **Instruments**: HST Advanced Camera for Surveys, VLT/MUSE integral field spectroscopy
- **Method**: High-resolution imaging with morphological classification and asymmetry quantification
- **Observable**: Morphological asymmetry parameter A_morph from geometric analysis
- **Precision**: Positional uncertainties ~0.01-0.1 arcsec depending on distance and surface brightness
- **Controls**: Point spread function effects, background contamination
