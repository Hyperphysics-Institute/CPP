### Physical Description
The Milky Way spiral structure exhibits systematic asymmetries in star formation rate, gas density, and stellar populations that correlate with spiral arm dynamics and galactic rotation. These asymmetries manifest as differences in star formation efficiency between upstream and downstream sides of spiral arms, asymmetric gas flow patterns around spiral shocks, and correlations between stellar age gradients and spiral arm geometry that exceed expectations from symmetric density wave theory, potentially indicating non-axisymmetric gravitational perturbations, magnetic field effects, or systematic observational biases in galactic structure mapping.

**Primary Observable**: Galactic spiral arm asymmetry A_spiral
