### Physical Description
Galactic cosmic rays exhibit systematic anisotropies in arrival direction that correlate with galactic coordinates, solar system motion through the local interstellar medium, and heliospheric magnetic field structure. These asymmetries manifest as dipole and higher-order multipole patterns in cosmic ray flux, temporal variations correlated with solar activity, and energy-dependent anisotropy amplitudes that exceed expectations from isotropic propagation models, potentially indicating local magnetic field effects, solar wind modulation asymmetries, or systematic detector response variations.
