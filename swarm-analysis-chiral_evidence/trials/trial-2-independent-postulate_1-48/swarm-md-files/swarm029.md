# Swarm029: Lunar Crater Ejecta Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in lunar crater ejecta blanket distribution and ray patterns
- **Scale**: Impact Cratering Physics (hypervelocity collisions)
- **Experimental Context**: High-resolution lunar orbital imaging and topographic mapping
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Fresh lunar impact craters exhibit systematic asymmetries in their ejecta blanket distributions that correlate with impact angle, target properties, and crater size. These asymmetries manifest as preferred directions in ray systems, asymmetric continuous ejecta extent, and correlations between ejecta asymmetry and crater rim morphology that exceed expectations from vertical impact models, potentially indicating oblique impact effects, target heterogeneity influences, or secondary crater formation processes.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Impact crater ejecta patterns on the lunar surface
- **Instruments**: Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera, Chang'e mission imaging, ground-based telescopes

- **Precision**: Spatial resolution ~0.5-2 meters per pixel depending on orbital altitude
- **Controls**: Lighting conditions, crater age effects, secondary modification processes

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Lunar crater ejecta asymmetry A_ejecta
- **Definition**: A_ejecta = (L_max - L_min)/(L_max + L_min) for maximum/minimum ejecta extent
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 5-10σ significance for well-preserved fresh craters
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric vertical impact predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻¹ to 10⁰ relative ejecta extent asymmetry depending on impact angle and target properties
