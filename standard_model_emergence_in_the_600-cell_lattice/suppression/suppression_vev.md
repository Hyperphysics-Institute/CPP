# VEV Volume Dilution: 1 / N_lattice⁴

## Overview
The vacuum expectation value (VEV) ⟨ϕ⟩ in CPP is the chiral condensate energy scale that couples to particle cages via Yukawa-like terms. It is suppressed from the Planck energy E_P ≈ 1.22 × 10^{28} MeV by a volumetric holographic factor 1 / N_lattice⁴, reflecting the 4-dimensional entropy of the 600-cell lattice.

## Geometric Origin
- The 600-cell tiles 4D space with cells containing exactly N_lattice = 120 vertices.
- Each cell is a finite, closed processor volume; SSV gradients and DI-bit flows are distributed across this volume.
- In 4D, information/entropy scales with volume ∝ N_lattice⁴ (analogous to area ∝ r² in 3D holography or volume ∝ r³ in non-holographic cases).
- Planck-scale energy density must therefore be diluted over the entire 4D cell volume before manifesting as an effective condensate at lower scales → suppression factor 1 / N_lattice⁴.

## Mathematical Form
⟨ϕ⟩ = k · E_P / N_lattice⁴ · ϕ_k  

where:  
- E_P ≈ 1.22 × 10^{28} MeV (Planck energy)  
- N_lattice = 120 → N_lattice⁴ ≈ 2.0736 × 10^8  
- ϕ_k = φ^k (golden-ratio layer factor, k = generational index)  
- k ≈ 0.0185 (universal calibration constant, fixed by electron mass)

Approximate raw dilution: 1 / 120⁴ ≈ 4.82 × 10^{-9}

## Role in Mass Generation
This factor sets the bridge from Planck-scale physics to TeV–MeV particle masses. Without it, VEV would remain near Planck energy → fermion masses near Planck scale (unphysical). With it, combined with k and ϕ_k, masses emerge in the observed range after Yukawa coupling to cages.

## Justification & Parsimony
- The exponent 4 is required by 4-dimensionality of the lattice—no choice.
- N_lattice = 120 is a fixed topological invariant of the 600-cell.
- The dilution is holographic (information bounded by surface/vertex count, not bulk volume), consistent with quantum gravity principles and the lattice's finite nature.
- k ≈ 0.0185 is overconstrained (charge neutrality, baryon stability, vacuum energy) and emerges approximately from 1/(N_lattice · φ²) before refinement—further evidence it is lattice-derived, not arbitrary.
