# ZBW Oscillation Cycle: SSV Gradient Flips

Zitterbewegung (ZBW) arises from the dynamic response of Dipole Pairs (DPs) to local Space Stress Vector (SSV) gradients. Each DP oscillates in a cycle of attraction and repulsion over two Planck times (t_Pl), driven by gradient flips.

## Step-by-Step Cycle
1. **Far Phase (Attraction Inward)**  
   When the +limb of a DP is distant from a central negative CP (or sea baseline), the SSV gradient is strongest inward (toward the central CP). The +limb moves toward the greatest gradient → attraction phase.

2. **Close Phase (Gradient Cancellation)**  
   As the +limb approaches the central negative CP, their SSVs point in opposite directions and sum toward zero (head-on cancellation). The net gradient in the inward direction drops to near zero.

3. **Flip Phase (Repulsion Outward)**  
   The +limb's "back" side now feels the stronger pull from the distant sea SSV (baseline gradient away from center). The gradient inverts: strongest outward → repulsion phase. The limb moves away.

4. **Cycle Reset**  
   As distance increases again, the inward gradient re-dominates → cycle repeats. Full period: 2 t_Pl (attraction + repulsion).

This is the trembling motion Dirac predicted, but in CPP it is real, sustained by SSV vector summation and lattice-constrained paths (FBS propagation along hyperedges, Appendix K). No external force—pure gradient chasing.
