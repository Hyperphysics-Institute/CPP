# W Boson: Linear Chain/Ribbon Structure (Appendix D.1)

The W boson is modeled as a linear chain of 6 hybrid Dipole Pairs (hDPs) forming a closed ribbon loop/doughnut, with a hole in the center. No central unpaired CP.

## Structural Details
- Composition: 6 hDPs (each hDP = eCP ↔ qCP pair), favoring hDP for strong-type mediation.
- Linear ZBW: Each DP oscillates back-and-forth along the chain, generating mass via kinetic energy.
- Open structure: Ends allow catalytic bonding with ±CP in weak processes (e.g., beta decay).
- W± formation: In high-energy conditions, sufficient SSV localizes energy to stabilize bond with real/unpaired ±CP → on-shell W± (mass from decay products).

## Mass Contribution
- E_ZBW from linear oscillations along chain.
- No inter-layer bonding (open ribbon) → lower organization than Z/Higgs.
- Observed: 80.379 GeV/c².

## Role
Catalyzes flavor changes (down → up) by nucleating W0 (neutral) → W± bond. Dissipates into sea after catalysis.
