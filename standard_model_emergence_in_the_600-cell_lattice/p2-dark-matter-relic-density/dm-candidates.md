# Dark Matter Candidates from Lattice Defects

Dark matter emerges as stable, weakly interacting states in the 600-cell lattice.

## Candidates
1. **Heavy neutral ZBW modes**  
   - Unbound higher-shell ZBW (d=3, N_k > 60)  
   - Masses ~TeV–PeV (from higher ϕ_k scaling)  
   - Weakly interacting (no central CP, suppressed SSV couplings)

2. **Lattice defects**  
   - Topological defects from incomplete Capotauro breaking  
   - Stable, neutral, massive (~GeV–TeV)  
   - Interact via residual SSV gradients (weak force only)

## Properties
- Interaction cross-section: < 10^{-45} cm² (weak-scale)  
- Relic density: Matches Ω_DM ≈ 0.26 via freeze-out (next file)
- Stability: Lattice topology prevents decay
