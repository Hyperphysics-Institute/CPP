\section{Statistical Justification: Binomial Concordance Test}
The primary statistical test used is the binomial probability of perfect concordance under the null hypothesis of no intrinsic chiral bias (random 50/50 left/right handedness per probe).

Of the 78 documented swarm entries, 60 are confirmed real physical observations (peer-reviewed astronomical/laboratory data). Observed: 60/60 in the same handedness direction.

Under the null ($p = 0.5$):
\[
P(X = 60 \mid n=60, p=0.5) = (0.5)^{60} \approx 8.7 \times 10^{-19}
\]

**Overlap correction**: Recognizing shared instruments/probes (CMB, JWST, DESI, etc.) across ~25–35% of entries, we conservatively reduce effective sample size to $n_{\text{eff}} \approx 42$:
\[
P(X = 42 \mid n=42, p=0.5) \approx (0.5)^{42} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{-13}
\]

This remains decisive evidence against the null. The binomial approach avoids assumptions about individual p-value precision and directly tests the key claim: uniform handedness across confirmed real observations.
