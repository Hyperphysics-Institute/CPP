## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

- **Scale**: Relativistic Astrophysics (black hole physics)

- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026

### Physical Description
Black hole accretion systems exhibit systematic asymmetries between approaching and receding relativistic jets that exceed expectations from simple Doppler beaming effects. These asymmetries manifest as differences in jet brightness, morphology, and particle acceleration efficiency between the two jet lobes, potentially indicating asymmetric accretion flow properties, black hole spin effects, or magnetic field configurations in the ergosphere region.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Relativistic jets from stellar-mass and supermassive black holes
- **Instruments**: Event Horizon Telescope, VLBA, Chandra X-ray Observatory, NuSTAR
- **Method**: Very long baseline interferometry and multi-wavelength jet imaging
- **Observable**: Jet brightness asymmetry A_jet
- **Precision**: Brightness ratio uncertainties ~10-20% depending on source and wavelength
- **Controls**: Doppler beaming corrections, interstellar scattering effects
