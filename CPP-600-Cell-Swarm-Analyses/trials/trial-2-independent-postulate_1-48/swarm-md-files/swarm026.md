# Swarm026: Exoplanet Atmospheric Terminator Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries between day-night terminator regions in exoplanet atmospheric composition and temperature
- **Scale**: Exoplanetary Atmospheric Physics (atmospheric circulation)
- **Experimental Context**: Transit and eclipse spectroscopy with space telescopes
- **Time Period**: 2016-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Hot Jupiter exoplanets exhibit systematic asymmetries between their eastern and western terminators during transit observations, manifesting as differences in atmospheric composition, temperature structure, and aerosol distributions. These asymmetries exceed expectations from simple day-night thermal gradients and correlate with planetary rotation rates and stellar irradiation levels, potentially indicating complex atmospheric circulation patterns, chemical transport processes, or magnetic field effects in tidally locked planetary atmospheres.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Tidally locked hot Jupiter atmospheres during primary transit
- **Instruments**: HST/WFC3, Spitzer/IRAC, JWST/NIRSpec early observations
- **Method**: Transmission spectroscopy with terminator-resolved atmospheric analysis
- **Observable**: Terminator asymmetry parameter A_term from spectral differences
- **Precision**: Spectral ratio uncertainties ~10⁻⁴ to 10⁻³ depending on system brightness
- **Controls**: Stellar activity, limb darkening effects, instrumental systematics
