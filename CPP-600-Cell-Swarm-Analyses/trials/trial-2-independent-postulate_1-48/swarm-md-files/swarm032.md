# Swarm032: Saturn Ring Particle Size Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in Saturn ring particle size distributions between leading and trailing ring sectors
- **Scale**: Planetary Ring Physics (collisional dynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: Cassini spacecraft observations and radio occultation measurements
- **Time Period**: 2015-2017 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Saturn's ring system exhibits systematic asymmetries in particle size distributions that correlate with orbital motion direction and ring density waves. These asymmetries manifest as differences in particle size frequency distributions between leading and trailing sectors of individual rings, variations in optical depth that correlate with particle flux directions, and correlations between asymmetry magnitude and proximity to shepherd moons, potentially indicating systematic collisional evolution, electromagnetic effects, or gravitational sorting processes.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Ice particles in Saturn's A, B, and C ring system
- **Instruments**: Cassini VIMS, UVIS, RSS radio occultation experiments
- **Method**: Multi-wavelength optical depth analysis and radio scattering measurements
- **Observable**: Ring particle size asymmetry A_ring from leading/trailing sector comparisons
- **Precision**: Optical depth uncertainties ~1-5% depending on ring region and wavelength
- **Controls**: Spacecraft geometry effects, ring opacity variations, temporal evolution
