# Swarm038: Nuclear Fission Fragment Angular Distribution Handedness
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic handedness preferences in nuclear fission fragment angular distributions
- **Scale**: Nuclear Physics (fission dynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: Thermal neutron induced fission experiments
- **Time Period**: 2016-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Uranium-235 thermal neutron fission exhibits systematic preferences in the handedness of fragment angular distributions that correlate with neutron beam direction and target nucleus orientation. These asymmetries manifest as reproducible directional preferences in the emission of fission fragments relative to the scission axis, correlations between fragment mass asymmetry and angular distribution handedness, and systematic deviations from mirror-symmetric fragment emission patterns, potentially indicating parity violation in strong interactions, nuclear structure effects, or systematic experimental asymmetries in detection geometry.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: U-235 fission induced by thermal neutrons
- **Instruments**: LANSCE neutron facility, Institut Laue-Langevin thermal neutron beams
- **Method**: Precision measurement of fission fragment angular distributions with handedness analysis
- **Observable**: Fission fragment handedness asymmetry A_fission from angular correlation analysis
- **Precision**: Angular distribution uncertainties ~1-5% depending on fragment energy and detector resolution
- **Controls**: Detector geometry systematic effects, neutron beam polarization, target alignment

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Nuclear fission handedness asymmetry A_fission
- **Definition**: A_fission = (N_right - N_left)/(N_right + N_left) for right/left-handed angular distributions
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-5σ significance with high-statistics fission measurements
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to mirror-symmetric fission predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻³ to 10⁻² relative handedness preference depending on fragment mass and kinetic energy
