- **Scale**: Substellar Physics (atmospheric dynamics)

### Physical Description
Brown dwarfs exhibit systematic hemispheric asymmetries in their atmospheric thermal structure and circulation patterns that manifest as photometric variability, spectroscopic line profile variations, and correlations between rotation period and atmospheric features. These asymmetries exceed expectations from simple radiative equilibrium models and show dependencies on spectral type and rotation rate, potentially indicating complex atmospheric dynamics, magnetic field effects, or patchy cloud distributions in substellar atmospheres.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Atmospheric circulation in substellar objects with masses 13-80 Jupiter masses
- **Instruments**: Spitzer Space Telescope, HST/WFC3, ground-based infrared spectrographs

- **Observable**: Atmospheric asymmetry parameter A_atm from variability analysis
- **Precision**: Photometric uncertainties ~0.1-1% depending on brightness and wavelength
- **Controls**: Instrumental systematic effects, stellar contamination for binary systems

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Brown dwarf atmospheric asymmetry A_atm
- **Definition**: A_atm = (T_max - T_min)/(T_max + T_min) for hemispheric temperature variations
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 4-8σ significance for well-characterized variable objects
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric radiative equilibrium predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻² to 10⁻¹ relative temperature asymmetry depending on rotation rate and atmospheric properties
