## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

- **Scale**: Planetary Atmospheric Physics (giant planet dynamics)

### Physical Description
Jupiter's Great Red Spot exhibits systematic asymmetries in its internal circulation structure that manifest as differences in wind velocities, vorticity distributions, and material transport between the northern and southern halves of the anticyclonic vortex. These asymmetries exceed expectations from axisymmetric vortex models and correlate with the spot's interaction with surrounding zonal jets, potentially indicating complex three-dimensional dynamics, vertical shear effects, or asymmetric forcing from deep atmospheric layers.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Long-lived anticyclonic vortex in Jupiter's southern hemisphere
- **Instruments**: Juno JunoCam imaging, HST/WFC3 monitoring, ground-based adaptive optics
- **Method**: Cloud tracking velocimetry and high-resolution morphological analysis
- **Observable**: Great Red Spot circulation asymmetry A_GRS from velocity field analysis
- **Precision**: Velocity uncertainties ~5-15 m/s depending on feature contrast and tracking baseline
- **Controls**: Projection effects, temporal evolution, instrument systematic effects
