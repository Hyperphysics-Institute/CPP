# Swarm043: Atmospheric Gravity Wave Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in atmospheric gravity wave propagation and breaking patterns between eastward and westward directions
- **Scale**: Atmospheric Physics (wave dynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: High-resolution atmospheric observations and radar measurements
- **Time Period**: 2016-2023 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Atmospheric gravity waves exhibit systematic asymmetries in propagation characteristics that correlate with background wind shear, stratospheric jet position, and topographic forcing. These asymmetries manifest as preferred propagation directions relative to the mean flow, differences in wave breaking altitudes between eastward and westward propagating waves, and correlations between wave momentum flux and directional preference that exceed expectations from linear wave theory, potentially indicating critical level filtering effects, nonlinear wave interactions, or systematic source asymmetries from orographic and convective forcing.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Atmospheric gravity waves in the middle atmosphere (10-100 km altitude)
- **Instruments**: AIRS satellite observations, ground-based radar networks, radiosondes
- **Method**: High-resolution temperature and wind measurements with wave spectral analysis
- **Observable**: Gravity wave directional asymmetry A_wave from momentum flux analysis
- **Precision**: Temperature measurement uncertainties ~1-2 K, wind speed ~2-5 m/s
- **Controls**: Instrument systematic effects, seasonal variations, background atmosphere removal

**Primary Observable**: Atmospheric gravity wave asymmetry A_wave
