# Swarm006: Galactic Cosmic Ray Anisotropy Patterns
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Large-scale anisotropies in galactic cosmic ray arrival directions
- **Scale**: Galactic Physics (cosmic ray propagation)
- **Experimental Context**: Ground-based extensive air shower arrays
- **Time Period**: 2015-2025 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Galactic cosmic rays exhibit significant large-scale anisotropies in their arrival directions, manifesting as systematic flux variations across the sky that exceed statistical expectations for isotropic distributions. These anisotropies reflect the combination of local galactic magnetic field structure, cosmic ray source distributions, and propagation effects through the interstellar medium, creating measurable left-right asymmetries relative to galactic coordinate systems.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: High-energy galactic cosmic rays detected via extensive air showers
- **Detectors**: Telescope Array, Pierre Auger Observatory, HAWC gamma-ray observatory
- **Method**: Sky map reconstruction with spherical harmonic analysis
- **Observable**: Dipole and quadrupole anisotropy amplitudes A_dipole, A_quad
- **Precision**: Statistical uncertainties ~10⁻⁴ to 10⁻³ with decade-scale datasets
- **Controls**: Detector exposure corrections, atmospheric systematic effects
