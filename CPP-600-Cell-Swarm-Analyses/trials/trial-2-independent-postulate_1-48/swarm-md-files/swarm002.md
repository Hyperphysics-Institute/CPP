### Physical Description
Cesium atoms exhibit parity violation in electronic transitions due to weak nuclear force interactions. The weak nuclear charge (Q_W) creates measurable left-right asymmetries in atomic energy levels, providing precision tests of electroweak theory at atomic scales.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Cesium-133 atoms in vapor cell
- **Transition**: 6S₁/₂ → 7S₁/₂ forbidden transition
- **Method**: Laser spectroscopy with circularly polarized light
- **Observable**: Asymmetry parameter A_PV in transition probability
- **Precision**: Sub-percent level measurements
- **Controls**: Systematic error analysis, magnetic field compensation

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Parity violation asymmetry A_PV
- **Definition**: A_PV = (σ₊ - σ₋)/(σ₊ + σ₋) for left/right circular polarizations
- **Statistical Power**: Typically >5σ significance achieved
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to electroweak predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻⁸ relative asymmetry
