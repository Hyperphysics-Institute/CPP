# Swarm048: Exoplanet Transit Light Curve Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in exoplanet transit light curve shapes between ingress and egress phases
- **Scale**: Exoplanet Physics (atmospheric dynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: High-precision photometric observations from space telescopes
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Exoplanet transit observations reveal systematic asymmetries in light curve morphology that correlate with planetary atmospheric properties, stellar activity, and orbital dynamics. These asymmetries manifest as differences in transit depth and duration between ingress and egress phases, correlations between asymmetry magnitude and atmospheric scale height, and systematic deviations from symmetric transit models that exceed photometric noise expectations, potentially indicating atmospheric circulation effects, stellar limb darkening variations, or tidal deformation of planetary atmospheres during transit.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Transiting hot Jupiter exoplanets with extended atmospheres
- **Instruments**: Kepler/K2 space telescope, TESS, HST/WFC3, JWST/NIRSpec
- **Method**: High-precision transit photometry with asymmetry analysis of light curve shape
- **Observable**: Transit asymmetry A_transit from ingress/egress timing and depth analysis
- **Precision**: Photometric precision ~10-100 ppm depending on star brightness and instrument
- **Controls**: Stellar activity corrections, instrumental systematics, limb darkening models

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Exoplanet transit asymmetry A_transit
- **Definition**: A_transit = (T_egress - T_ingress)/(T_egress + T_ingress) for transit timing asymmetry
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-5σ significance for well-observed hot Jupiter transits
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric circular transit predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻³ to 10⁻² relative timing asymmetry depending on atmospheric properties
