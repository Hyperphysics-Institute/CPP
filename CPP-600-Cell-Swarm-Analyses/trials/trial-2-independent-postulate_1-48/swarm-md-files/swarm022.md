# Swarm022: Pulsar Wind Nebula Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Morphological and spectral asymmetries in pulsar wind nebula structure relative to pulsar motion
- **Scale**: Pulsar Physics (relativistic magnetized winds)
- **Experimental Context**: High-resolution X-ray and optical imaging
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Pulsar wind nebulae exhibit systematic morphological asymmetries that correlate with pulsar proper motion direction and magnetic field orientation. These asymmetries manifest as bow-shock structures, asymmetric tails, and spectral index variations between leading and trailing edges that exceed expectations from simple spherical wind models, potentially indicating anisotropic pulsar wind injection, magnetic field reconnection effects, or interaction with asymmetric supernova remnant environments.

### Experimental Setup

- **Precision**: Positional uncertainties ~0.1-1 arcsec depending on distance and brightness
- **Controls**: Point spread function effects, background subtraction
