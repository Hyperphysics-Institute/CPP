## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information

- **Scale**: Planetary Volcanism Physics (tidal heating)

- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026

### Physical Description
Io's volcanic plumes exhibit systematic asymmetries in their ejection patterns that correlate with the moon's orbital position, tidal stress field, and surface topography. These asymmetries manifest as preferred plume directions relative to Jupiter, differences in plume height and particle density between leading and trailing hemispheres, and correlations between plume asymmetry and volcanic vent orientation that exceed expectations from simple gas-driven eruption models, potentially indicating complex subsurface magma dynamics, atmospheric circulation effects, or electromagnetic interactions with Jupiter's magnetosphere.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Sulfur dioxide and sulfur volcanic plumes on Jupiter's moon Io
- **Instruments**: Juno JunoCam, HST/WFC3, ground-based adaptive optics telescopes
- **Method**: High-resolution imaging with plume morphology and trajectory analysis
- **Observable**: Volcanic plume asymmetry A_plume from directional ejection analysis
- **Precision**: Plume height uncertainties ~10-50 km depending on observation geometry
- **Controls**: Orbital phase effects, atmospheric density variations, instrument resolution

**Primary Observable**: Io volcanic plume asymmetry A_plume
