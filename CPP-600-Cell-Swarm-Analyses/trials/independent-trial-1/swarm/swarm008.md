# Swarm008: Stellar Magnetic Field Polarity Preferences
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Statistical preferences in stellar magnetic field polarity orientations across stellar populations
- **Scale**: Stellar Physics (magnetohydrodynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: Spectropolarimetric surveys of stellar magnetic fields
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Main sequence and evolved stars exhibit systematic preferences in their magnetic field polarity orientations that exceed expectations from random field generation processes. These asymmetries manifest as statistical correlations between magnetic field polarity and stellar rotation axis orientation, potentially indicating underlying dynamo mechanisms or primordial field inheritance that creates preferred magnetic configurations in stellar populations.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Stellar photospheric magnetic fields across diverse stellar types
- **Instruments**: ESPaDOnS/CFHT, HARPS-Pol, Zeeman Doppler Imaging surveys
- **Method**: Spectropolarimetric magnetic field measurements and statistical analysis
- **Observable**: Magnetic polarity asymmetry A_pol across stellar samples
- **Precision**: Field strength uncertainties ~1-10 Gauss depending on stellar type
- **Controls**: Stellar activity cycle corrections, projection geometry effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Stellar magnetic polarity asymmetry A_pol
- **Definition**: A_pol = (N_positive - N_negative)/(N_positive + N_negative) for field polarities
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 2-4σ significance with large stellar samples
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to random polarity distribution
- **Scale**: ~10⁻² to 10⁻¹ relative polarity preference depending on stellar mass range
