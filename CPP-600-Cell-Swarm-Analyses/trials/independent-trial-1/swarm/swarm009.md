### Physical Description
Planetary atmospheres across the solar system exhibit systematic hemispheric asymmetries in their circulation patterns that exceed expectations from simple Coriolis-driven symmetric models. These asymmetries manifest as differences in jet stream strengths, storm formation rates, and thermal structure between northern and southern hemispheres, potentially indicating complex interactions between atmospheric dynamics, seasonal forcing, and planetary magnetic field effects.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Atmospheric circulation patterns on Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, and Venus
- **Instruments**: Cassini CIRS/VIMS, Juno microwave radiometer, Mars Climate Sounder
- **Method**: Thermal wind measurements and circulation pattern analysis
- **Observable**: Hemispheric circulation asymmetry A_circ
- **Precision**: Wind velocity uncertainties ~1-5 m/s depending on altitude and planet
- **Controls**: Seasonal variation corrections, instrument systematic effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Hemispheric circulation asymmetry A_circ
- **Definition**: A_circ = (U_north - U_south)/(U_north + U_south) for equivalent latitudes
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-6σ significance with multi-year datasets
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric circulation models
- **Scale**: ~10⁻¹ to 10⁰ relative circulation strength asymmetry depending on planet and season
