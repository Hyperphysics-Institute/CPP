### Physical Description
Core-collapse supernovae exhibit systematic asymmetries in their neutrino emission patterns that correlate with the explosion geometry and stellar rotation. These asymmetries manifest as preferred neutrino emission directions relative to the stellar rotation axis and magnetic field orientation, potentially indicating asymmetric collapse dynamics, convective instabilities, or magnetic field effects during the explosion mechanism.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Neutrino emission from core-collapse supernova explosions
- **Detectors**: Super-Kamiokande, IceCube, SNEWS network, Hyper-Kamiokande preparation
- **Method**: Multi-detector neutrino arrival time and direction analysis
- **Observable**: Neutrino emission directional asymmetry A_ν
- **Precision**: Direction uncertainty ~5-15 degrees depending on detector and neutrino energy
- **Controls**: Detector systematic effects, neutrino oscillation corrections
