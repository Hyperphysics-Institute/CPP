### Physical Description
Quasar jets exhibit systematic asymmetries in precession behavior that correlate with black hole spin direction, accretion disk orientation, and jet Lorentz factor. These asymmetries manifest as preferred helical twist handedness in jet structure, correlations between precession sense and radio polarization patterns, and systematic differences in jet opening angles that exceed expectations from symmetric relativistic jet models, potentially indicating frame-dragging effects from rotating black holes, magnetic field helicity transfer from accretion disks, or systematic observational biases in VLBI imaging.
