# Swarm019: Type Ia Supernova Explosion Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in Type Ia supernova explosion geometry and ejecta distribution
- **Scale**: Stellar Explosion Physics (thermonuclear burning)
- **Experimental Context**: Optical/NIR spectroscopy and spectropolarimetry
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Type Ia supernovae exhibit systematic asymmetries in their explosion geometry that manifest as polarization signatures, line profile asymmetries, and ejecta velocity differences between different viewing angles. These asymmetries exceed expectations from spherically symmetric explosion models and correlate with host galaxy properties, potentially indicating asymmetric ignition mechanisms, binary companion interactions, or rotation effects in the progenitor white dwarf systems.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Type Ia supernova explosions in nearby galaxies
- **Instruments**: Keck/LRIS spectropolarimetry, VLT/FORS2, HST follow-up observations
- **Method**: Multi-epoch spectroscopy and polarimetry with geometric modeling
- **Observable**: Explosion asymmetry parameter A_exp from polarization and line profiles
- **Precision**: Polarization uncertainties ~0.1-0.5% depending on supernova brightness
- **Controls**: Interstellar polarization corrections, host galaxy dust effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Type Ia explosion asymmetry A_exp
- **Definition**: A_exp = √(Q² + U²)/I for Stokes parameters, or line profile asymmetry measure
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-6σ significance for well-observed nearby events
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to spherically symmetric explosion predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻¹ to 10⁰ relative asymmetry in ejecta velocity or polarization depending on epoch
