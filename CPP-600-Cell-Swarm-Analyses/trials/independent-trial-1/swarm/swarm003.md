# Swarm003: Neutrino Beam Muon Decay Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Angular distribution asymmetries in muon decay from neutrino beam interactions
- **Scale**: Particle Physics (weak interaction)
- **Experimental Context**: Long-baseline neutrino experiments
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Muons produced in neutrino beam interactions exhibit systematic asymmetries in their subsequent decay angular distributions. These asymmetries manifest as preferred emission directions for decay products relative to the original neutrino beam axis, providing sensitive probes of weak interaction physics beyond minimal Standard Model expectations.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Muons from charged-current neutrino interactions in detector targets
- **Detectors**: T2K near detector complex, NOvA near detector, MINERvA
- **Method**: Precision tracking of muon decay electron/positron trajectories
- **Observable**: Forward-backward asymmetry A_FB in muon rest frame
- **Precision**: Statistical uncertainties ~0.1-0.3% with large event samples
- **Controls**: Detector acceptance corrections, systematic uncertainty analysis

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Muon decay forward-backward asymmetry A_FB
- **Definition**: A_FB = (N_forward - N_backward)/(N_forward + N_backward) relative to beam axis
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-4σ significance with full datasets
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to V-A weak interaction predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻³ to 10⁻² relative asymmetry depending on kinematic selection
