# Swarm005: Cosmic Ray Muon Atmospheric Depth Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: East-west asymmetries in cosmic ray muon flux measurements at varying atmospheric depths
- **Scale**: Atmospheric Physics (cosmic ray interactions)
- **Experimental Context**: Ground-based and underground cosmic ray detectors
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Cosmic ray muons produced in atmospheric showers exhibit systematic east-west flux asymmetries that correlate with atmospheric depth and geomagnetic field orientation. These asymmetries arise from the combination of primary cosmic ray rigidity cutoffs, atmospheric geometry effects, and the Earth's magnetic field deflection of charged primaries, creating measurable directional preferences in secondary muon production.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Cosmic ray muons from atmospheric shower cascades
- **Detectors**: IceCube surface array, HAWC observatory, Pierre Auger ground stations
- **Method**: Directional muon flux measurements with atmospheric depth correlation
- **Observable**: East-west flux asymmetry A_EW as function of zenith angle
- **Precision**: Statistical uncertainties ~0.5-1% with multi-year datasets
- **Controls**: Atmospheric density corrections, geomagnetic field modeling

**Primary Observable**: East-west muon flux asymmetry A_EW
- **Definition**: A_EW = (Φ_east - Φ_west)/(Φ_east + Φ_west) for equivalent zenith angles
