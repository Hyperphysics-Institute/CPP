# Swarm023: White Dwarf Magnetic Field Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Systematic asymmetries in magnetic field structure and strength across white dwarf stellar surfaces
- **Scale**: Stellar Physics (degenerate matter magnetism)
- **Experimental Context**: Spectropolarimetric observations of magnetic white dwarfs
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Magnetic white dwarfs exhibit systematic asymmetries in their surface magnetic field distributions that deviate from simple dipolar configurations. These asymmetries manifest as hemisphere-to-hemisphere differences in field strength, complex multipolar components, and correlations between field topology and stellar rotation that exceed expectations from fossil field evolution models, potentially indicating dynamo processes, field decay mechanisms, or crystallization effects in degenerate stellar cores.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Surface magnetic fields of isolated magnetic white dwarf stars
- **Instruments**: VLT/FORS2 spectropolarimetry, Keck/LRIS-P, HST/COS UV observations
- **Method**: Zeeman spectropolarimetry with magnetic field mapping techniques
- **Observable**: Magnetic field asymmetry A_B between stellar hemispheres
- **Precision**: Field strength uncertainties ~0.1-1 MG depending on field strength and line quality
- **Controls**: Stellar rotation phase, instrumental polarization effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: White dwarf magnetic field asymmetry A_B
- **Definition**: A_B = (⟨|B|⟩_north - ⟨|B|⟩_south)/(⟨|B|⟩_north + ⟨|B|⟩_south) for hemispheric averages
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 3-6σ significance for strong-field systems with good phase coverage
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric dipole field predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻¹ to 10⁰ relative field strength asymmetry depending on field complexity
