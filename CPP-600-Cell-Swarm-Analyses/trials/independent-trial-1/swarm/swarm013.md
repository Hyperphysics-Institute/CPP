### Physical Description
Quantum vacuum exhibits measurable birefringence in the presence of strong magnetic fields, causing systematic asymmetries in light polarization states during propagation. These asymmetries manifest as differential phase accumulation between orthogonal polarization modes, providing direct tests of quantum electrodynamics predictions for vacuum polarization effects and potential signatures of physics beyond the Standard Model.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Polarized light propagation through magnetized vacuum or neutron star magnetospheres
- **Instruments**: PVLAS laboratory setup, neutron star X-ray polarimetry (IXPE, XPOLARIMETER)

- **Observable**: Vacuum birefringence asymmetry A_bire
- **Precision**: Polarization angle sensitivity ~10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁹ radians depending on setup
