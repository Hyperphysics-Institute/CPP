## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

- **Scale**: Exoplanetary Physics (atmospheric dynamics)

### Physical Description
Exoplanet transits exhibit systematic asymmetries between ingress and egress phases that exceed expectations from simple geometric occultation models. These asymmetries manifest as differences in light curve slope, depth variations, and timing offsets that correlate with planetary atmospheric properties, potentially indicating atmospheric circulation patterns, temperature gradients, or magnetic field interactions with stellar winds.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Transit light curves of hot Jupiters and super-Earths
- **Instruments**: TESS, Kepler/K2, ground-based photometric networks
- **Method**: High-precision differential photometry with asymmetry parameter extraction
- **Observable**: Transit asymmetry parameter A_transit
- **Precision**: Photometric uncertainties ~10-100 ppm depending on system brightness
- **Controls**: Stellar variability corrections, instrumental systematic effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: Transit ingress-egress asymmetry A_transit
- **Definition**: A_transit = (τ_egress - τ_ingress)/(τ_egress + τ_ingress) for transit durations
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 2-4σ significance for well-characterized systems
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to symmetric geometric transit models
- **Scale**: ~10⁻³ to 10⁻² relative timing asymmetry depending on atmospheric properties
