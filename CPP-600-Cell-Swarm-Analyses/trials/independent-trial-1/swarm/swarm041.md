# Swarm041: Earthquake Rupture Propagation Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Statistical rupture direction bias in strike-slip earthquakes relative to regional stress field orientation
- **Scale**: Seismic Geophysics (fault dynamics)
- **Experimental Context**: Global seismic networks and InSAR displacement measurements
- **Time Period**: 2015-2023 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Strike-slip earthquakes exhibit systematic asymmetries in rupture propagation direction that correlate with regional stress field orientation, fault geometry, and tectonic loading history. These asymmetries manifest as statistical preferences for rupture propagation in specific directions relative to the maximum horizontal stress axis, correlations between rupture directivity and fault slip rate, and systematic differences in stress drop between forward and backward rupture directions that exceed expectations from symmetric fault mechanics models, potentially indicating pre-stress heterogeneity, fault zone asymmetry, or dynamic rupture effects.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: Strike-slip earthquake ruptures on continental transform faults
- **Instruments**: Global Seismographic Network, InSAR satellites (Sentinel-1, ALOS-2)
- **Method**: Seismic source inversion and InSAR displacement field analysis with rupture directivity characterization
- **Observable**: Earthquake rupture asymmetry A_rupture from directivity analysis
- **Precision**: Rupture direction uncertainties ~10-30 degrees depending on network geometry and magnitude
- **Controls**: Station distribution effects, velocity model uncertainties, rupture complexity
