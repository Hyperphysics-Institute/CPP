## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information

- **Scale**: Planetary Atmospheric Physics (convective vortices)
- **Experimental Context**: High-resolution orbital imaging and surface weather monitoring
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Martian dust devils exhibit systematic asymmetries in their track orientations, widths, and morphological features that correlate with local topography, seasonal patterns, and atmospheric circulation. These asymmetries manifest as preferred track directions relative to prevailing winds, asymmetric track widths between upslope and downslope segments, and correlations between vortex rotation sense and track characteristics that exceed expectations from simple convective models, potentially indicating complex atmospheric dynamics, Coriolis effects, or terrain-induced circulation patterns.

### Experimental Setup

- **Precision**: Track width measurements ~1-5 meters depending on image resolution
- **Controls**: Seasonal variations, regional topographic effects, image acquisition timing
