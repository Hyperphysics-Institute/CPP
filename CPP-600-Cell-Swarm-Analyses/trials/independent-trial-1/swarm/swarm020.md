# Swarm020: Gamma-Ray Burst Jet Break Asymmetries
## Left-Right Asymmetry Analysis

### Study Information
- **Phenomenon**: Asymmetric jet break features in gamma-ray burst afterglow light curves
- **Scale**: Relativistic Astrophysics (ultra-relativistic jets)
- **Experimental Context**: Multi-wavelength GRB afterglow observations
- **Time Period**: 2015-2024 measurements
- **Selection Date**: January 24, 2026
- **Selected By**: Claude (Swarm)

### Physical Description
Gamma-ray burst afterglows exhibit systematic asymmetries in their jet break signatures, where the characteristic steepening in light curve decay differs between observing frequencies and shows correlations with burst duration and spectral properties. These asymmetries manifest as frequency-dependent jet break times and slopes that exceed expectations from standard fireball models, potentially indicating asymmetric jet structures, viewing angle effects, or complex shock physics in ultra-relativistic outflows.

### Experimental Setup
- **System**: GRB afterglow emission from relativistic jet interactions with circumburst medium
- **Instruments**: Swift XRT/UVOT, Fermi LAT, ground-based optical/radio telescopes
- **Method**: Multi-wavelength light curve analysis with broken power-law fitting
- **Observable**: Jet break asymmetry A_break between different wavebands
- **Precision**: Break time uncertainties ~10-30% depending on data quality and sampling
- **Controls**: Host galaxy extinction, instrument systematic effects

### Expected Asymmetry Measurement
**Primary Observable**: GRB jet break asymmetry A_break
- **Definition**: A_break = (t_break,ν₁ - t_break,ν₂)/(t_break,ν₁ + t_break,ν₂) for different frequencies
- **Statistical Power**: Typically 2-4σ significance for well-sampled multi-wavelength afterglows
- **Direction**: Empirical measurement compared to standard fireball model predictions
- **Scale**: ~10⁻¹ to 10⁰ relative break time asymmetry depending on jet structure and viewing angle
